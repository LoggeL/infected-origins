# Infected Origins — Motivation Fix: Why abduct Frank, infect him, and kill witnesses?

## Problem identified by Logge

The B + C takeover idea creates a continuity/motivation issue with the main film:

- Why would Lilith/GenetiX abduct Frank?
- Why intentionally infect him?
- Why kill people to protect the project?
- If she hates GenetiX, why continue GenetiX-style cruelty?

This needs to be solved or the prequel will feel disconnected from the main film.

---

## Strongest fix

The project is not “evil for profit” anymore. Under Lilith, it becomes a desperate containment/cure program with monstrous methods.

Her logic:

> “The virus already exists. If it gets out, everyone dies. If I stop, my father’s victims died for nothing. If I continue, I might still turn it into a cure.”

This makes her protective secrecy make sense.

She does not protect the project because she loves GenetiX.
She protects it because she believes the project is now the only thing standing between humanity and the thing GenetiX created.

---

## Why abduct Frank?

Frank should not be random. He needs to be scientifically necessary.

Possible reason:

- Frank has a rare genetic marker that makes him unusually compatible with the serum.
- Earlier subjects died too quickly or mutated uncontrollably.
- Frank is the first adult subject whose body can survive long enough to produce useful data.
- His blood may be needed to stabilize the cure for the mother or reverse the virus.

Lilith's justification:

> “If Frank dies untreated, one man dies. If Frank survives the protocol, millions may live.”

This is the same moral math her father used with the abducted child.

---

## Why infect Frank?

Because the serum/virus cannot be understood safely from samples anymore. It needs a living host.

Better version:

- Frank is not infected for fun or pure weapon testing.
- He is infected because Lilith believes a controlled living infection is the only way to map the full progression and develop a stabilizer/antidote.
- He becomes “Patient Zero” for the stabilized strain.
- The black veins are the visible sign that the virus is both working and failing.

Lilith's justification:

> “The disease is already in the world. I need one controlled infection before there are thousands uncontrolled.”

This makes her still horrifying, but not random.

---

## Why kill witnesses / protect the project?

Because exposure creates three disasters at once:

1. **Public panic:** Nobody will understand the nuance; the project will be shut down before a cure exists.
2. **Containment risk:** Anyone who had contact with Frank may be infected or carrying traces.
3. **Legal/ethical collapse:** The truth would reveal abductions, deaths, and GenetiX crimes, ending the only lab capable of stopping the virus.

Lilith's twisted logic:

> “A witness is not just a witness. A witness is a leak, a carrier, and the end of containment.”

This directly supports the main film's brutality: the hunt is not only about secrecy, but also about containment paranoia.

---

## Why continue after hating GenetiX?

Because she separates the corrupt company from the research.

Her self-lie:

> “GenetiX created the monster. I am the only one who can control it.”

But in practice, she rebuilds the same machine:

- secret lab
- guards
- abductions
- containment killings
- dehumanized subjects
- no witnesses

That is the tragedy: she kills the old GenetiX leadership, then becomes a more disciplined version of GenetiX.

---

## Recommended continuity spine

1. Father researches cure for mother.
2. GenetiX corrupts the research into serum/virus experimentation.
3. Father orders an abduction for a compatible subject.
4. Daughter discovers the truth; father dies in the confrontation.
5. GenetiX board incident happens; one board member or executive survives infected/altered.
6. Lilith realizes the virus cannot simply be destroyed because samples, subjects, and data are already spread through GenetiX systems.
7. She takes control not as CEO fantasy, but as emergency containment.
8. Frank is abducted later because he has the rare marker needed for stabilization.
9. Frank escapes.
10. Main film hunt begins: no witnesses, no spread, protect the cure/project at any cost.

---

## Key line for Lilith

> “You think I’m protecting GenetiX. I’m protecting you from what GenetiX made.”

This can be sincere and still monstrous.
