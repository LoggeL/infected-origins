# Infected Origins — Scene-by-Scene Outline

Goal: tell the transformation without skips. The daughter/Lilith change should feel gradual, not sudden.

## Scene 1 — Zuhause: Der gute Grund

Warm home scene. The doctor cares for his sick wife. The daughter helps with household and research notes. We see love, exhaustion, and hope. The Lilith mythology image is visible in the background but not explained yet.

Purpose: establish that the family is real and the research begins from love.

## Scene 2 — Die Tochter als Partnerin

Father and daughter work together on a formula/logbook late at night. She notices something he missed. He is proud of her. The mother watches with mixed warmth and concern.

Purpose: establish that the daughter is scientifically capable and emotionally invested.

## Scene 3 — Die Mutter warnt

The mother has a private moment with the daughter. She says she wants to live, but not at any cost. She references the Lilith image: a woman punished for refusing control, but warns that rebellion can also become pride.

Purpose: give the mother active moral weight and plant the Lilith symbol.

## Scene 4 — Wissenschaftliche Demütigung

The doctor presents his research to a board/conference/university committee. They reject him harshly. Some laugh, some are genuinely concerned about ethics. His paper/funding is denied.

Purpose: wound his pride while keeping the critics partly right.

## Scene 5 — GenetiX öffnet die Tür

After the humiliation, GenetiX approaches him. They validate him: “They laughed because they are afraid you might be right.” They offer labs, funding, speed, and no public ridicule.

Purpose: make GenetiX seductive, not cartoonishly evil.

## Scene 6 — Der erste Erfolg

In the GenetiX lab, tests improve. The wife shows a small positive response or a lab result suggests hope. The daughter is impressed but cautious. The doctor feels alive again.

Purpose: show why staying with GenetiX is tempting.

## Scene 7 — Erste Grenzüberschreitung

GenetiX pushes him to use questionable samples or skip protocols. He protests, but they frame it as urgency. He agrees to “one exception.”

Purpose: first moral compromise.

## Scene 8 — Türen schließen sich

At home, the daughter asks questions. He evades. At the lab, files are passworded. He stops explaining. He says: “Das verstehst du noch nicht.” It hurts because she does understand.

Purpose: show the relationship changing visibly.

## Scene 9 — Die Forschung kippt

The compound behaves less like a cure and more like a virus/serum: aggressive reactions, black-vein symptoms, unstable hosts. GenetiX becomes more interested in control and deployment than healing.

Purpose: transform the project before the father fully admits it.

## Scene 10 — Mutter erkennt den Preis

The mother confronts him gently or painfully. She realizes he is not only fighting for her anymore. She says something like: “Wenn du dafür jemand anderen opferst, rettest du mich nicht.”

Purpose: make his later betrayal of her wishes clear.

## Scene 11 — Der Point of No Return: Entführung

GenetiX presents a target with a rare compatible marker. The doctor hesitates because the target is innocent/young. Under pressure, fear, and obsession, he authorizes the abduction: first indirectly, then clearly enough to be responsible.

Purpose: his irreversible fall; direct bridge toward the main-film world.

## Scene 12 — Die Tochter findet die Wahrheit

The daughter investigates: hidden files, patient profiles, video logs, or transport orders. She learns the research now includes abduction and human experimentation. She also sees that her father signed/approved it.

Purpose: move her from suspicion to moral certainty.

## Scene 13 — Labor-Konfrontation

She confronts him in the lab. The argument centers on the mother, the abducted subject, and his wounded pride.

Key exchange idea:

- “Du hast gesagt, du rettest Mama.”
- “Das tue ich.”
- “Dann warum hast du ein Kind bestellen lassen?”

Purpose: emotional climax of father/daughter relationship.

## Scene 14 — Unfalltod des Vaters

She tries to destroy evidence, steal samples, or stop the test. He tries to prevent her. In the struggle, he dies by accident: exposure, fall, equipment failure, or impact. His last words can be ambiguous: “Lass sie es nicht nehmen.”

Purpose: tragedy, not murder. His final line can poison her interpretation.

## Scene 15 — GenetiX rekrutiert sie

GenetiX covers up the death and approaches her. They need her because she knows the research and can read/continue her father’s work. She acts devastated but useful. She plays along.

Purpose: make her succession practical and strategic.

## Scene 16 — Die Maske sitzt

She performs loyalty: colder language, sharper clothes, less emotion. She speaks in GenetiX terms: subjects, containment, stability. The audience should sense she is acting, but also that part of her likes the control.

Purpose: transition from daughter to future Lilith without a sudden jump.

## Scene 17 — Vorstandspräsentation läuft aus dem Ruder

She gathers board/executives for a closed demonstration. Her stated goal: prove the stabilized compound and decide the project’s future. Her hidden goal: expose or trap them. A surviving board figure/security chief panics and triggers lockdown or tries to kill her. The virus is released.

Purpose: B + C version — not a clean murder fantasy, but an escalation where her choice matters.

## Scene 18 — Der echte Kipppunkt: Sie rettet sie nicht

She reaches the emergency console. She could open containment or administer a countermeasure. Instead, she keeps the room sealed. Some die. One may survive infected/altered as future internal threat or proof of the virus.

Purpose: her first conscious monstrous decision. Not killing from rage, but choosing containment/control over mercy.

## Scene 19 — Lilith wird geboren

After the incident, she sees or remembers the Lilith image/name. She adopts it as identity: not victim, not daughter, not tool. She frames herself as the one who will control what GenetiX created.

Purpose: self-baptism into the main-film antagonist.

## Scene 20 — Übernahme als Notfall, nicht Triumph

She secures data, locks down the lab, gives orders to guards, rewrites the narrative. She does not celebrate. She says/acts: “GenetiX created this. I will contain it.”

Purpose: explain why she keeps the project alive.

## Scene 21 — Frank wird notwendig

A later briefing identifies Frank as a rare compatible host needed to stabilize, map, or reverse the infection. She authorizes his capture/infection under the logic of controlled containment.

Purpose: connect directly to the main film and make Frank scientifically necessary.

## Scene 22 — Jagdprotokoll

Final bridge: green serum prepared, Frank in OP gown/file, white van, armed guards, containment rules. She issues the doctrine: escaped subjects must be recovered; witnesses are contamination risks.

Purpose: land exactly in the main film’s world.

## Final thematic shape

- Father begins with love and becomes monstrous through fear, humiliation, and obsession.
- Daughter begins as moral witness and becomes monstrous through grief, control, and inherited purpose.
- Both tell themselves they are preventing something worse.
